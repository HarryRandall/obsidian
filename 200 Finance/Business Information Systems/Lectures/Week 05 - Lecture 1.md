## Key Concepts

### 1. Methodologies

- **Design thinking** - A human-centered approach to innovation
- **Waterfall** - A linear, sequential approach to software development
- **Agile methodologies** - An iterative and incremental approach emphasising flexibility and collaboration

## Examples

> [!example] Design Thinking
> A methodology focused on understanding user needs and creating innovative solutions.

> [!example] Waterfall vs Agile
> Waterfall follows a sequential process, while Agile uses iterative cycles.

---

## Formulas & Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Design Thinking | A human-centered approach to innovation that draws from the designer's toolkit to integrate the needs of people, the possibilities of technology, and the requirements for business success |
| Waterfall | A linear, sequential approach to software development where each phase must be completed before the next begins |
| Agile | An iterative and incremental approach to software development that emphasises flexibility, collaboration, and frequent delivery of working software |
